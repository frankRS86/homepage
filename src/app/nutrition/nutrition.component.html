Basics about calories and macros

There is one simple truth you must know which is the basic for all upcoming infos about nutrition:

If you want to gain muscle and therefore weight, you must consume more calories than you burn

If you want to lose weight, you must consume less calories than you burn

Thats all. Simple right?
To find out how many calories you burn a day, there are several methods:

  • use a daily calories calculator of your choice like the one below
  • use your smartwatch with calory-counter
  • go to the pysiotherapist of your trust and make a test

You know how many calories you burn on an avarge day? Perfekt. Now about the foods you eat, what they consist of and how many calories they contain. There are three basic macro nutrition elements everything eadible on the planet consist of. And they occur in different percantages per food. But most important, each macro has a different amount of calories per gram:

  • Carbohydrates - 4 calories per gram
  • Fats - 9 calories per gram
  • Protein - 4 calories per gram
With this knowledge, you are able to calculate exactly how many calories you eat a day, or to make a meal plan for your muscle-gaining diet.

Basic food knowledge

You know now how many calories each macro contains but does this mean you can saturate your daily colory needs by 100% fat intake? Well theoretically yes, but this might not be the best idea. Consider the following:

  • Your body needs all of these macros (and consisting micros, like vitamines) to stay healty and to function well.
  • carbohydrates deliver important and quick energy for your body to keep high performance. So you should plan with around 50% of calories through carbs a day.
  • In order to grow muscle your daily protein intake should be 2g per Kg bodyweight. Means if you weight 70kg, you should aim for 140g of protein a day.
  • Your body also needs a certain amount of fats a day to stay healthy (especially unsaturated fats like omega3). So eating no fats at all to lose bodyfat, is no good idea.
Also there are differences in the quality of each macro:
  • Good carbs:brown rice, lentils, beans
  • Bad carbs:white bread, sugar, soda, sweets
  • Good fats:mono- / poly-unsaturated fats like fish, nuts, avocado, eggs (yes), olives
  • Bad fats:staturated-/ trans-fats like in meat, dairy products, fast-food, palm-oil
  • Good source of protein:lentils, beans, nuts, eggs, peas, soy, lean meat, fish, fat reduced dairy products
  • Not so good source of protein:fat meat (bacon, minced meat), fat cheese
